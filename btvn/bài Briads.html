Briards

A HEART WRAPPED IN FUR

The briard, or berger be, is a large breed of dog traditionnally used as a herder and guardian of sheep.

Breed History

The briard, which is believed to have originated in France, has been bred of centuries to herd and to protect sheep. The breed was used by the
French Army as sentries, messngers and to search for wounded soldiers because of its fine sense of hearing. Briards were used in the First
Word War almost to the point of extinction. Currently the population of briards is slowly recovering. Charlemagne, Napoleon, Thomas Jefferson and Lafayette all owned briards.
